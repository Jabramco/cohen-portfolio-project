Passover

Every year during the Jewish holiday Passover, families across the world come together to celebrate an amazing story and observe an ancient tradition.

What's Passover?

Passover is a fun Jewish holiday that takes place every Spring. It's a festival and lasts seven to eight days! The holiday commerates the story of a group of Israelites who freed themselves from Egyptian slavery.

What's a Haggadah?

The Haggadah is the book read during Passover. It has the story of Passover and is read every year during the Seder.

What's a Seder?

The Seder is the ceremony where everyone gathers to tell the story of Passover and sing, eat, and pray together. President Obama attended a Seder!

What's Matzah?

During Passover, you get to eat Matzah! Matzah is bread that didn't have time to rise so it is flat. It's kind of like a giant cracker. It's a huge part of the Passover story.

The Four Questions

The youngest child in the family will read (or sing) the four questions. They are called the four questions, but really are four responses to one overarching question.

Why is this night different from all other nights?
  • On all other nights we eat leavened products and matzah, and on this night only matzah.
  • On all other nights we eat all vegetables, and on this night only bitter herbs.
  • On all other nights, we don’t dip our food even once, and on this night we dip twice.
  • On all other nights we eat sitting or reclining, and on this night we only recline.
